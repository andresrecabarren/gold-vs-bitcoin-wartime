# Gold vs. Bitcoin in Wartime Contexts

### From Historical Safe Haven to Escape Infrastructure

---

> **«Before the war, the market buys shelter.  
> During the war, people buy an exit.»**

---

## About This Paper

This briefing note explores a fundamental distinction that financial markets tend to overlook: the difference between a **theoretical safe-haven asset** and an **operational safe-haven asset** when institutional order actually breaks down.

The core thesis is that **Bitcoin does not compete with gold as "digital gold"** — it competes as a monetary technology designed for scenarios where wealth needs to become **portable, self-custodied, and resistant to territorial blockage**.

Gold was, for millennia, humanity's answer to the risk that power would degrade money.  
Bitcoin may be the answer to a different risk: **that power would block the person**.

## Key Ideas

- **Store of value ≠ Continuity of value** — Preserving purchasing power over time is not the same as preserving economic agency under disruption.
- **Volatility ≠ Fragility** — An asset can be volatile in price yet highly antifragile in function under coercion.
- **The market reasons by historical analogy** — When geopolitical risk rises, capital flows to gold by reflex. But when war becomes reality, the relevant properties of an asset shift from narrative to logistics.
- **Gold protects wealth from degradation; Bitcoin can also protect it from immobilization** — They serve complementary, not identical, functions.
- **Hard money may need a broader definition** — Not just difficult to produce, but also difficult to confiscate, easy to self-custody, and possible to transport without friction.
